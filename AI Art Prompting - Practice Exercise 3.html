Part 1: Parameter Deep Dive

This is your reference guide. Understand these tools to gain full control over your Midjourney creations.

Core & Technical Parameters

--ar: Sets aspect ratio (e.g., 16:9).
--v: Sets model version (e.g., 6, 7).
--seed: Reuses a composition for consistency.
--style raw: Reduces Midjourney's default aesthetic for a more literal result.
--stylize [0-1000]: Controls how artistic Midjourney gets. Low is literal, high is creative.
--chaos [0-100]: Controls the variety and unpredictability of the initial image grid.
--quality [value]: Adjusts rendering quality/time. (e.g., .25, .5, 1).
--stop [10-100]: Stops generation early for a less-finished look (e.g., --stop 50 for a painterly sketch).
--tile: Creates a seamless, repeating pattern.

Reference Parameters

--sref <URL> (v6+): Applies a reference image's style. Use --sw to adjust strength.
--cref <URL> (v6+): Makes a character look like a reference. Use --cw to adjust what is copied (face vs. full outfit).
--oref <URL> (v7+): Inserts a specific object/creature from a reference. Use --ow to adjust strength.

Interactive & Advanced Tools

Keyword Weights: Emphasize concepts like so: a cat ::2 and a dog ::1.
Interactive Tools: Use Pan, Zoom, and Vary (Region) after upscaling to expand or fix an image.

Part 2: Prompt Evolution Projects (Choose any Six)

Project 1: Cinematic Character Design (Focus: --cref, --cw, --sref)

Hop 1: Concept

Prompt a "suave gambler hero."

full-body portrait of a suave gambler hero in an elegant tuxedo, cinematic lighting --ar 2:3 --v 6

Hop 2: Lock Face

Use --cref with a reference image URL to lock in the hero's face.

... --cref <URL_of_hero_face>

Hop 3: Change Costume

Put the hero in "rugged adventurer's gear" but keep the face.

Use --cw 0.
...in rugged adventurer's gear... --cref <URL_of_hero_face> --cw 0

Hop 4: Apply Style

Apply a "film-noir" style from a reference image, making it dominate the look.

Use --sref and --style raw.
... --sref <URL_of_film_noir_art> --style raw

Project 2: Game Asset Consistency (Focus: --oref, --ow, --v 7)

Hop 1: Design Asset

Create a "jeweled lucky coin" icon.

Use --ar 1:1 and --v 7.
a jeweled lucky coin with a four-leaf clover --ar 1:1 --v 7

Hop 2: Place in Scene

Use --oref to place that exact coin into a new scene: "a coin spinning on a poker table."

a close-up of a coin spinning on a poker table --v 7 --oref <URL_of_lucky_coin>

Hop 3: Adjust Blending

The coin looks slightly pasted on. Use --ow to make it blend more naturally.

Lower the --ow value.
... --oref <URL_of_lucky_coin> --ow 80

Project 3: Exploring Creativity (Focus: --chaos, --stylize)

Hop 1: The Base Prompt

Start with a simple concept: "knight on horseback."

knight on horseback --ar 3:2

Hop 2: Introduce Low Chaos

Add a small amount of variation to the initial grid.

knight on horseback --ar 3:2 --chaos 10

Hop 3: Embrace High Chaos

Push the creativity with high chaos for experimental, unexpected results.

knight on horseback --ar 3:2 --chaos 80

Hop 4: Add High Stylization

Combine high chaos with high stylization to give Midjourney maximum artistic freedom.

A high --stylize value (e.g., 750) encourages beautiful, artistic results.
knight on horseback --ar 3:2 --chaos 80 --stylize 750

Project 4: Controlling Composition (Focus: Keyword Weights)

Hop 1: A Balanced Scene

Create a scene with three elements: "a dragon, a knight, and a castle."

a dragon fighting a knight in front of a castle --ar 16:9

Hop 2: Emphasize the Dragon

Use keyword weights to make the dragon the dominant subject.

Use the syntax dragon::2.
a dragon::2 fighting a knight in front of a castle --ar 16:9

Hop 3: Shift Focus to the Knight

Now make the knight the main focus, with the dragon as a secondary element.

a dragon::1 fighting a knight::2 in front of a castle --ar 16:9

Hop 4: Make the Castle a Backdrop

Keep the focus on the fight and de-emphasize the castle, making it more of a background detail.

Give the castle a lower weight, like 0.5.
a dragon::1.5 fighting a knight::1.5 in front of a castle::0.5 --ar 16:9

bioluminescent jungle vines, glowing neon flora --ar 1:1 --tile --stop 50

Project 7: Technical Rendering Control (Focus: --quality, --stop)

Hop 1: The Detailed Scene

Create a complex scene: "ultra-realistic close-up of a roulette wheel spinning."

ultra-realistic close-up of a roulette wheel spinning, sharp focus, cinematic detail --ar 1:1 --v 6

Hop 2: Fast Draft Quality

Render a quick, low-quality version for fast iteration.

Use --quality .25 or --q .25.
... --q .25

Hop 3: High Quality Render

Now render it at the default, high quality setting.

... --q 1

Hop 4: Painterly Stop

Render the image again, but stop it midway to create an artistic, painterly effect.

... --q 1 --stop 70

Hop 5: Sketchy Stop

Finally, create a very early, sketch-like version of the scene.

... --q 1 --stop 30

Project 8: World Building with Pan & Zoom (Focus: Interactive Tools)

Hop 1: The Focal Point

Create a single "ornate ancient temple slot machine."

Use --ar 9:16 to emphasize its height.
an ornate ancient temple slot machine, hieroglyphic details --ar 9:16

Hop 2: Reveal Surroundings

After upscaling, which tool reveals the environment around the machine?

Answer: The "Zoom Out" buttons.

Hop 3: Expand the World

Now with a wider view, which tool extends the scene to the right to show a "long hallway"?

Answer: The "Pan Right" (➡️) button.

Hop 4: Add a Detail

After panning, a section of the wall is blank. How would you use Vary (Region) to add "ancient hieroglyphs carved into the wall"?

Answer: Upscale the panned image, click "Vary (Region)", select the blank wall, and prompt "ancient hieroglyphs carved into the wall."
